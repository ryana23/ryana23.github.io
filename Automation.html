Data Automation: Geoduck Harvest Compliance Monitoring

Washington's Department of Natural Resources manages the statewide wild geoduck harvest (pronounced gooey-duck). Our compliance officers are always on-site making sure that the harvest is done safely and within the terms of our harvest contract.

The dive officers have collected these data for decades in paper and digital logs, which is a difficult dataset to analyze and to use in compliance investigations. My solution to making these data usable to our staff scientists and managers was to develop an automated field data collection process.

We use Survey123 for field data collection and Microsoft Power Automate for cloud automation. This process runs continuously. It manages data, creates reports, and sends updates to field staff through email.

Now, after 7 years in use, this dataset has more than 3,700 daily records! Each contains every event from every vessel, from every harvest area, with every GPS location, for every boat. More than 54,000 actions in total!
