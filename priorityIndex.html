Sonar Priority Mapping

DNR staff need updated maps and imagery so that their decisions protect Washington State's resources. Aquatics' science group and I work together to collect spatial data on marine and freshwater habitats. We use sonars to map the seafloor, drone aerial cameras to map beaches and the sea surface, GPS to map habitat edges and resources, and water quality instruments to track physical properties.

We use an online project tracking list for our land managers and staff to submit data needs. We then rank which projects we can completed given the limited amount of field time available. In the application, a user clicks the area they need data and then selects the "prioritize here" button.

The "prioritize" link has a custom URL that takes the location of the grid cell and records the latitude and longitude into an ArcGIS Survey123 form. The simple online form can be filled out by staff and recorded on the priority web map.
