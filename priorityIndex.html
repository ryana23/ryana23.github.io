Sonar Priority Mapping

DNR staff need updated maps and imagery so that their decisions protect Washington State's resources. I work with Aquatics' science group to gather spatial data on marine and freshwater habitats. We use various technologies to map different areas. Sonar allows us to map the seafloor. Drone cameras capture images of beaches and the sea surface. GPS helps us outline habitat edges and resources. Water quality instruments track physical properties.

We use an online project tracking list for our land managers and staff to submit data needs. We rank the projects we can complete based on our limited field time. In the application, a user clicks the area they need data for and then selects the "prioritize here" button.

The "prioritize" link uses a special URL. It grabs the grid cell's location and sends the latitude and longitude to an ArcGIS Survey123 survey. The simple online form can be filled out by staff and recorded on the priority web map.
